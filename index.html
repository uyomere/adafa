ABOUT US

Advancing Development in Africa For Africans was founded in 2022, Its an organization for pioneering the advancement of development in Africa, focusing on six major aspects: Governance, Funding, Education, Water, Climate Action, and Technology. These aspects have four major drivers: ADAFA, Government, NonGovernmental Organizations, and Companies. ADAFA have its operational headquarters in Nigeria. The organization was necessitated due to the social exclusion of nearly 95% of Africans, this can be seen in most African countries with few adequately educated and only few have access to clean water, only few exercise their human rights, and very few have knowledge about technological inventions.

WATER

Water is a precious yet non-renewable resource. Yet in Africa, the same water can be a source of life and death. Water is not only the most basic of need but also at the center of sustainable development and essential for poverty eradication. Water is intimately linked to health, agriculture, energy and biodiversity. However, water should not be a constraint to development for Africa appears to have abundant water resources. It has large rivers, big lakes; vast water lands and limited, but widespread groundwater resources. Moreover, it has a high potential for the development of hydroelectric power. Unfortunately, the sustainability of these water resources cannot be taken for granted for it is threatened by certain natural phenomena and human factors. Without progress on water, reaching other Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) will be difficult if not impossible and the fight against poverty will remain a pipe dream.

CLIMATE ACTION

Climate change is a real and undeniable threat to our entire civilization. Africa has the world's lowest per capita greenhouse gas emissions, but is one of the hardest hit continents in terms of climate change impacts. Africa's poorest communities are often powerless in the face of extreme weather events such as droughts and floods, while the famine and the livelihood insecurity that ensue are frequently catastrophic. The effects are already visible and will be catastrophic unless we act now. It’s therefore essential that we contributes to alleviating this pressure on the continent. Through education, innovation and adherence to our climate commitments, we can make the necessary changes to protect the planet. The climate crisis is a global crisis and what happens in Africa is felt by the rest of the world.
